# Decisions

## D-001: Companion First

Desktop Cat is a companion product. It should prioritize calm presence over technical spectacle.

## D-002: V1 Is Lightweight

V1 must stay small and shippable. Complex systems move to the roadmap.

## D-003: No Growth System In V1

Levels, experience, intimacy scores, and other growth mechanics are postponed.

## D-004: Cartoon World Style

The cat should be translated into a unified cartoon desktop world. The goal is to preserve the real cat's strongest traits, not to copy the photo realistically.

## D-005: Demo Cat

The first demo cat is a short-haired black cat with golden eyes.

## D-006: Desktop World

The cat lives on the desktop, can move freely, and can be dragged by the user. V1 does not require entering application windows.

## D-007: Launch At Login

Launch at login should be a setting. It may default to on during later product design, but the user must be able to disable it.

## D-008: Speech Bubbles

V1 may use small speech bubbles. Motion and behavior should communicate first; text should be occasional and quiet.

## D-009: Replaceable AI Service

AI image generation must be accessed through a replaceable provider layer. Internal alpha can use the founder's API key.

## D-010: PAC Workflow

Founder gives natural-language direction. Product Architect converts it into project decisions and tasks. Codex implements.

## D-011: Checkpoint Rhythm

After about 10 meaningful product conversations, summarize decisions, update project documents, review project health, and place creative ideas in the backlog.
